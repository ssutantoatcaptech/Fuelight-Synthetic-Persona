Fuelight 360: Synthetic Persona Review

Stress-testing the Bulk Edit Side Panel (CXP & Market) through 5 grounded personas before scheduling user-facing research. Identifying gaps in assumptions, surfacing design problems, and defining clear next steps for moderated and unmoderated testing.

Assessment date: July 16, 2026

Initial Ask

Before investing in moderated or unmoderated user testing, stress-test the Bulk Edit Side Panel (CXP & Market) through synthetic personas grounded in real Fuelight discovery interviews. Identify what problems are worth solving and what's missing — so sessions are focused on validating, not discovering.

Feature: Quick Edit (Bulk Edit Side Panel) Testing: Moderated sessions in progress · Unmoderated TBD Method: Tier 1 — Synthetic (AI persona review, internal)

The Core Design Gap

Thesis The Bulk Edit panel presents data but not decisions. Every persona — regardless of seniority, literacy, or workflow — needs the same thing the tool doesn't provide: context that turns a number into a judgment call. "Is this good? Should I act? Can I defend it?"

The tool solves the mechanical problem (change numbers in bulk) without solving the decision problem (why these numbers, what does it mean, and can I defend it). It's built for one user type: someone who already knows exactly what to change. Even that user finds it slow.

Method

This assessment follows the Tier 1: Synthetic research method — an internal practice using AI persona agents grounded in real Fuelight discovery quotes to stress-test designs before investing in user-facing sessions.

Steps Taken

  1. Feature audit — Read full source (~5,700 lines each) of Side Panel CXP and Market to document every control, interaction, and post-apply behavior
  2. Persona profiles — Extracted all 5 personas from the Persona Lab: roles, decision routines, pain points, data literacy, and Fuelight expectations
  3. Framework mapping — Grouped personas into 3 tiers: Executive Decision Makers, Operational Managers, Analytical Power Users
  4. Stress test — Ran each persona through the feature: "What would they do? What confuses them? What's missing?"
  5. Cross-reference — Built gap matrix identifying shared themes vs. persona-specific issues
  6. Synthesis — Distilled into patterns, challenges, and prioritised design principles

Common Patterns

1

Everyone needs the "so what" — not just the "what changed"

Every persona needs to understand the meaning of edits in their own terms. The tool stops at the mechanical layer and never rises to the narrative layer.

2

Nobody trusts a number they can't trace back to a reason

The tool lets you apply +10% but never asks or records why +10%. It's a calculator without a receipt.

3

The tool is a single-session scratch pad, not a planning system

Scenarios live in sessionStorage and disappear on refresh. Can't name, share, compare, or build on them over time.

4

Speed vs. rigour is a false trade-off the design doesn't resolve

Marco needs 3 clicks. Camila needs a full audit trail. These aren't opposed — they're different moments in the same workflow. Let Marco apply fast, let Camila audit later.

Design Principles (Derived from Gaps)

1

Every number needs a reference point

Show vs. target, vs. last year, vs. plan — never just the raw value.

2

Every edit needs a rationale

Capture why, not just what. Even a one-line text field transforms auditability.

3

The operator and the decision-maker see the same data differently

Build one table, two modes: edit mode (Marco) and review mode (Daniel/Camila).

4

Speed and rigour are sequential, not competing

Fast to apply (Marco), auditable after the fact (Camila).

5

The tool should produce an artifact, not just a state

Scenarios need names, exports, and shareable summaries — not just sessionStorage.

Testing Plan: Moderated Sessions

When to use Executive Decision Makers (Daniel, Camila) and complex Analytical workflows (Sarah, Elena). These users need follow-up probing to understand why they react the way they do.

Session Structure (45 min)

  1. Context setting (5 min) — Brief on scenario: "You're preparing for the Monthly Business Review. A colleague applied these bulk edits yesterday."
  2. Task 1: Comprehension (10 min) — "Look at this table. What happened? Is the scenario on track?"
  3. Task 2: Action (15 min) — "Apply a +5% adjustment to Digital Media for Coca-Cola across Q3. Walk me through your thinking."
  4. Task 3: Validation (10 min) — "Would you sign off on this scenario for IC submission? What's missing?"
  5. Debrief (5 min) — "What would you change about this experience?"

Hypotheses to Validate

  • H1: Users cannot determine "on track vs. off track" from the current table without mental arithmetic
  • H2: The 5-step wizard creates friction that discourages repeated use
  • H3: Executives will not use the output for IC without a narrative summary
  • H4: Finance will block adoption without assumption transparency

Testing Plan: Unmoderated Sessions

When to use Operational Managers (Marco) and repeat-use workflows. These users reveal friction through time-on-task and error rates, not verbal explanation.

Task Script (Maze/UserTesting.com)

  1. Task 1: "Find the YTG value for Coca-Cola Digital Media. Is it above or below the annual target?" (measures: can they find it? do they know if it's good?)
  2. Task 2: "Increase Digital Media spend by 5% for July and August only." (measures: time-on-task, error rate, abandonment)
  3. Task 3: "You just applied a rule. What changed? Summarize in one sentence." (measures: comprehension of post-apply state)
  4. Task 4: "Apply the same rule you just used, but for Sprite this time." (measures: discoverability of repeat/template flow)

Success Metrics

  • Task 2 completion in under 90 seconds (current estimate: 3+ minutes)
  • Task 3 accuracy: user can correctly state the direction of change
  • Task 4: fewer than 50% of users re-configure from scratch (indicates template need)
  • SUS score above 68 (current predicted baseline: 55-60)

How to Use This Log

1

After each real test

Fill 3-5 rows per session. Mark "Correct" predictions too — they validate the persona model.

2

Monthly review (or every 5 sessions)

Filter by Delta ≠ Correct. Look for repeating patterns per persona. Update profiles in Persona Lab.

3

Quarterly scoring

Score each persona: % Correct out of total predictions. Below 60% accuracy = structural rewrite needed.

4

The compounding effect

Each real test makes the next synthetic review more accurate. The gap between prediction and reality is the learning.
